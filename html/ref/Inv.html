Inv

Inv[pops, inv]
calculates the growth rate of invader inv invading the resident community pops.

Inv[traits, pops, Guildguild]
calculates the growth rate of an invader in guild guild (default=first), using resident trait values traits.

Inv[traits, pops, traitinv]
uses invader traits traitinv.

DetailsDetails

  • The identity of the invader is specified as a variable (for unstructured pops), as a name (for structured pops), as a Guild (first guild if omitted), or as a list of traits.
  • When a Guild is given, the invader traits are denoted with subscript 0.
  • Based on the model type and inputs, Inv has three modes: "Eigenvalue", "ContinuousTime Floquet", and "DiscreteTime Floquet".
  • "Eigenvalue"(model is unforced [Period = 0] AND pops is not a function of time) OR (model is forced [Period != 0] AND (Method = "Instantaneous" OR Time is given))
    "ContinuousTime Floquet"ModelType is "ContinousTime" AND ((model is forced [Period != 0] AND Method != "Instantaneous" AND Time is not given) OR pops is a function of time)
    "DiscreteTime Floquet"ModelType is "DiscreteTime" AND ((model is forced [Period != 0] AND Method != "Instantaneous" AND Time is not given) OR pops is a function of time)
  • "Eigenvalue" mode first solves for quasi-steady state equilibria of intensive components (if any), then plugs these into equations of extensive components, which are assumed to be zero. In the case of only one extensive component, invasion fitness is calculated as the per capita growth rate. In the case of more than one extensive components, invasion fitness is the dominant eigenvalue of the Jacobian matrix of extensive component equations.
  • "ContinuousTime Floquet" first solves for quasi-steady state cycles of intensive components (if any), then plugs these into equations of extensive components, which are assumed to be zero. In the case of only one extensive component, invasion fitness is calculated as the per capita growth rate averaged over one period or the length of the pops given. In the case of more than one extensive components, invasion fitness is the dominant Floquet exponent of the Jacobian matrix of extensive component equations.
  • "DiscreteTime Floquet" is similar, where invasion fitness is the product of growth rates (1 component) or the dominant eigenvalue of the product of Jacobian matrices of extensive component equations (2+ components), raised to the 1/n power, where n is the number of time steps.
  • The following options can be given:
  • Method"Automatic"method to calculate invasion rate
    Timetfix time if nonautonomous model
    NIntegrateOpts{Method -> {Automatic, "SymbolicProcessing" -> 0}}options to pass to NIntegrate
    IntegrateOpts{}options to pass to Integrate
    NDSolveOpts{}options to pass to NDSolve
    SolveOpts{}options to pass to Solve
    NSolveOpts{}options to pass toNSolve
    FindRootOpts{}options to pass to FindRoot
    EigensystemOpts{}options to pass to Eigensystem
    FindEcoCycleOpts{}options to pass to FindEcoCycle
    QSSMethod"NSolve"method to solve for intensive components in quasi-stable state
    QSSICs"Automatic"initial guess to solve for intensive components in quasi-stable state
    SimplifyResultFalsehow to simplify results
    Guild"Automatic"guild invader
    VerboseFalseprint out extra info
    VerboseAllFalseapply Verbose recursively
  • Possible settings for the Method option relevant to the case of a single extensive component in mode "Continuous Floquet" are:
  • "Automatic"method "NIntegrate"
    "NIntegrate"use NIntegrate
    "Integrate"use Integrate
    "NDSolve"use NDSolve
  • traits and pops can be combined in traitsandpops.
