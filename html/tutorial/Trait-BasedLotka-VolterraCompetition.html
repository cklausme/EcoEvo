Trait-Based Lotka-Volterra Competition

The trait-based Lotka-Volterra competition model considers competing species (ni) with a single trait (xi) that determines carrying capacity and the strength of competition. It is the canonical example used in adaptive dynamics.

    

k[xi] describes the trait-dependence of the carrying capacity and a[xi,xj] describes how the strength of competition depends on the difference between species traits. Typically we assume that k[xi] is unimodal and a[xi,xj] is a symmetric function of xi-xj and declines as the trait difference increases. For example,
